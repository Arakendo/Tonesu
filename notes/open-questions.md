- [ ] **Evidentiality.** The Cultural Precision Domain 1 (epistemic states) raises a natural follow-on: does Tonesu grammatically mark the *source* of knowledge? - [ ] **Epistemic deflation as a distinct charge.** `to-fe-ka` covers both inflation (se presented as to) and deflation (to-su suppressed as si). The evidentiary burden differs: proving inflation requires showing the claim was mislabeled; proving deflation requires establishing what status a claim had *actually reached*. May need either a qualifier or a separate compound when corpus produces a formal adjudication sentence where the direction (inflation vs deflation) matters legally.
- [ ] **The manufactured-doubt problem.** Deflation `to-fe-ka` is structurally adjacent to legitimate conservative epistemic judgment. A `to-fe-li` panel adjudicating a deflation charge must distinguish three positions that look identical from the outside: (1) genuine conservative judgment — "I don't think this has reached to-su" — not a violation; (2) `to-fe-ki` — honest misjudgment of the threshold; (3) `to-fe-ka` deflation — knowing the threshold was met, suppressing the promotion. This three-way distinction is *only resolvable objectively* if `to-fe-su` (the standards framework) defines promotion thresholds procedurally. Without `to-fe-su`, every deflation charge collapses into a subjective dispute and the `to-fe-li` role cannot adjudicate. **PARTIALLY RESOLVED by C005 B3:** `to-fe-su` now has its first corpus attestation and has been registered as W072. The institution is now in the language. The manufactured-doubt problem remains the functional design challenge: the procedural threshold definition is the content of `to-fe-su`, not of this registry entry. See W072 notes.

An unpublished standard is adoption without certification; institutional capture shows up precisely here — a body that enforces rules it has not published to `to-su` status is exercising unchallengeable authority. The distinction may eventually need a compound or qualifier. Candidate: `to-fe-su-ki` (the inchoative — the moment a standards rule enters published `to-su` status) vs bare `to-fe-su` (the body / the unpublished ruleset). **C005 B3 now makes this active:** the routing sentence says "submit to `to-fe-su`" — implying the body's rulings have some kind of published/unpublished status. - [ ] **`zo-si` vs `zo-to` disambiguation — keep distinct.** `zo-si` = living-signal = spirit / disembodied agent (an entity that exists as signal without physical substrate). `zo-to` = living-pattern = soul / identity-pattern of a living being (the organizing conceptual structure constituting personal identity). S084 used `zo-to` for the soul-as-claim inside an epistemic frame; S085 used `zo-si` as a non-physical agent. Both worked cleanly. **Do not collapse into one compound.** The distinction is the same as `zo-si`: entity without body vs `zo-to`: organizing pattern of a body-having entity. If Concordian speakers repeatedly need both concepts in adjacent sentences, consider registering both. Current status: unregistered candidates.
- [ ] **Magic-primitive trap — principle log.** Design principle extracted from session: premature mystical primitives (mana, spirit, soul, divine) cause three failure modes: (1) the concept later proves to be a subtype of an existing primitive (mana ≈ `ra`); (2) the single primitive collapses multiple distinct concepts that later need separating; (3) the worldbuilding changes and the ontology becomes inconsistent. Tonesu avoids all three by treating mystical/religious constructs as compounds, domains, and cultural registers. **The language encodes no metaphysical commitment about whether souls and spirits are real.** A sentence like `la-yo  si  lo-mu  zo-to` asserts a doctrine claim; it does not assert the ontological reality of `zo-to`. The language is metaphysically neutral by design. Primitive threshold for any metaphysical root: corpus pressure + ontological necessity (the setting requires distinguishing the mystic substrate from `ra`, `wi`, `to`, and `ne` simultaneously, with no compound path that resolves the distinction).
