- [ ] **Content question word.** C001 exposed the first content question ("what kind?"). Workaround: `to-si` (W026, query) used as floating interrogative placeholder — "the thing I am seeking knowledge of." This is compositionally transparent and requires no new particle. Confirm this as the canonical strategy: `[topic]  [dimension]  to-si?` = "what [dimension] is [topic]?" — or whether a dedicated question pronoun is needed. Do not invent in advance; collect 2–3 more content questions first.
- [ ] **Utterance-level epistemic modal.** C001 Turn A3 established the pattern `la-[speaker]  [epistemic-root]  [proposition]` = "I hold [proposition] at epistemic level [root]." E.g., `la-mi  si  [fe-ki-now]` = "I assess as signal-level: it crosses the limit now." This is structurally distinct from the evidentiality candidates (`se-ro`/`si-ro`/`to-ro`, which mark *how* you know). The epistemic modal marks *what status you assign the proposition*. Both mechanisms may coexist. Formalize in spec/grammar.md § Epistemic Modality once a second corpus case confirms the pattern.
- [ ] **Agent drop / speaker drop in conversation.** C001 Turn B3 attested `la-mi` being omitted when the speaker is the obvious actor (`ka-ki-now` = "[I'm] going now"). Provisional rule: in face-to-face exchange, the current speaker may be dropped from the agent slot when the action can only be theirs. This is separate from imperative agent-drop (which drops `la-tu`) and argument-drop-by-context (`ki-now` with no agent in C001 Turn A2). Three distinct ellipsis patterns may be in play; formal taxonomy needed. Relate to topic-drop open question above.
- [ ] **Affirmative and negative response particles.** C001 managed without a standalone "yes." B3 used action-confirmation (`ka-to-su-ki` = understood) and motion-declaration (`ka-ki-now` = I'm going) where English would use "yes." Open question: (a) does Tonesu have no bare affirmative/negative — agreement is always expressed through action-type? or (b) does a minimal affirmative/negative compound exist but not yet appear in corpus? The distinction matters for dialogue structure. Log; collect more conversation data.
- [ ] **P-QM-001: Polar question marker — no current solution.** C003 A1 is the first yes/no question in the corpus. Current workaround: `to-si` fronting, which was established for content questions (C001 B1). Both uses are semantically coherent under `to-si` = "seeking information," but the structures differ: content questions have an unknown argument within the embedded proposition; polar questions have an unknown truth-value of a complete proposition. Whether one particle handles both or a dedicated polar marker is needed depends on whether a genuinely ambiguous corpus case arises. Candidate markers: (a) retain `to-si` as a universal question frame; (b) develop `to-si` for content questions and a separate ending/post-propositional particle for polarity questions. Do not decide until corpus shows stress on the current solution. Expected within 20–30 more sentences of dialogue.
- [ ] **P-AF-001: Minimal affirmative particle — mounting pressure.** Three conversation turns now work around "yes": C001 B3 (action-commit: `ka-to-su-ki` / `ka-ki-now`), C003 B1 (echo-confirmation: full proposition restated from B's perspective), and C004 A3 (institutional routing: neither agreeing nor disagreeing). Two of three are productive strategies; echo-confirmation is the closest analog to "yes." However, echo-confirmation is verbose in fast dialogue and fails when the question doesn't have a simple agent-switch (`la-tu` → `la-mi`). The language will likely develop a minimal affirmative. Candidate root: `ru` (unity/coherence — "[it is] coherent" = yes) or a new phonological particle. Do not register until corpus forces it.
- [ ] **`la-[process]  be  [proposition]` evidential frame — formalize.** C004 A2 used `la-se-ka  be  [lo-mu  de]` = "the examination produces [the finding]: the machine has decayed." An inanimate process (`se-ka`) occupies the `la` agent-slot and generates an embedded proposition as its output via `be` (produce). This is a new grammatical construction: `la-[epistemic-process]  be  [embedded-prop]` = "the [process] produces the finding that [prop]." Distinct from the person-epistemic-modal pattern (`la-[person]  [epistemic-root]  [prop]`). Two classes of epistemic predication are now emerging: person-based (assertion) and process-based (evidence). Formalizing this distinction in spec/grammar.md is worth doing once a second process-epistemic frame appears in corpus.
- [x] ~~**`no-` as universal negation prefix — formalized.**~~ **Resolved.** Spec written into spec/morphology.md § Productive Prefixes. Three depth classes confirmed: (1) primitive root prefix (`no-de`, `no-ru`, `no-fe`, `no-ha`); (2) compound predicate prefix (`no-ne-fe`, S063 — decisive structural case); (3) clause negation `no [ka-clause]` provisional (attested S036, one more case needed before full grammar rule). `no-` scope is the entire predicate form, not only the final morpheme. Negation section in spec/grammar.md expanded with three-level summary table.
- [ ] **P-GP-001: `vo` and `ru` predication — agent slot vs patient slot.** S030 used `la-to-su-mu  vo` (agent-slot subject, quality predicate) and S031 used `la-li-pu  ka  ru` (quality as post-verbal manner modifier). Both patterns place a primitive quality root (`vo`, `ru`) in a predicate role, but both differ from the established copula-by-juxtaposition pattern (`lo-X  state`), which uses the patient slot. Three distinct predication strategies are now in corpus: (1) `lo-X  state` = "X is in [state]" (patientive); (2) `la-X  quality` = "X has/exhibits [quality]" (attributive/possessive); (3) `ka  quality` = "the action has [quality]" (manner-adverbial). These may be one unified copula-adjacent construction or three separate grammatical things. Do not collapse prematurely. Resolve when corpus produces three or more contrastive pairs.
- [ ] **P-GP-002: `re` aspect vs time adverbial — deferred but closing.** S029 Attempt A uses `ta-re-ti` (time-particle + recurring-time noun) to express habituality: a time-adverbial strategy. S029 Attempt B (`re-fe-ki`, `re` as verb prefix) expresses the same meaning as morphological aspect on the verb. Both are compositionally legal. The language must eventually decide: (a) aspect is expressed only via time adverbials (`ta-re-...`); (b) aspect is a verb prefix (`re-VERB`); or (c) both strategies are legal with distinct nuances. The decision point arrives when a corpus sentence requires the difference — e.g., a single-event vs habitual reading of the same verb and time context. S029 Attempt A is canon for now; Attempt B is held provisional.
- [ ] **P-GP-003: `go`/`du` bilateral clause coordination — no boundary rule exists.** S028 Attempt 1 tried `go [clause]  du [clause]` as a fully biclausal coordination frame. The established boundary rule (spec/grammar.md § Subordinate Clauses) covers `go [subordinate clause]  [matrix continues]`, which works because the matrix follows. A symmetric two-frame construction — `go [clause-A]  du [clause-B]` — has no matrix clause: A and B are coordinate peers. The question is whether this is (a) legal as a new biclausal coordination construction with its own boundary rule, or (b) not allowed (must use S011-style noun-compound abbreviated form: `go-[cause]  du-[result]`). The single-frame abbreviated form (S028 Attempt 2) is canon for now. Attempt 1 is held as a pending grammatical hypothesis.

## Primitives and Ontology

- [x] ~~Is `li` too broad?~~ → Decided: `li ⊂ zo`. Every `li` is a `zo`. Animals are `zo` only. AI/institutions are `mu + li`. See registry/primitives.md entity model.
- [x] ~~`ki` overloaded?~~ → Narrowed: `ki` = motion only. General transformation uses `be`/`de` compounds. Candidate root `ce` (state change) pending corpus evaluation.
- [ ] Does `to` need to split into `thought` and `knowledge`? Working model: `to` = conceptual pattern/thought; `to+su` = organized knowledge (body of knowledge); `to+si` = encoded information / data. Confirm before corpus work scales — S013 used `to` alone for "knowledge", which may be too loose.
- [ ] **Evidentiality.** The Cultural Precision Domain 1 (epistemic states) raises a natural follow-on: does Tonesu grammatically mark the *source* of knowledge? Many languages with rich epistemic vocabulary develop light evidentiality markers — lexical tools that specify how a speaker came to know something. Candidates using existing roots: `se-ro` (by direct perception), `si-ro` (by received signal / hearsay), `to-ro` (by inference/reasoning). Do not add until corpus shows a need to distinguish these in-sentence — one confirmed ambiguous corpus case first.
- [ ] **Epistemic deflation as a distinct charge.** `to-fe-ka` covers both inflation (se presented as to) and deflation (to-su suppressed as si). The evidentiary burden differs: proving inflation requires showing the claim was mislabeled; proving deflation requires establishing what status a claim had *actually reached*. May need either a qualifier or a separate compound when corpus produces a formal adjudication sentence where the direction (inflation vs deflation) matters legally.
- [ ] **The manufactured-doubt problem.** Deflation `to-fe-ka` is structurally adjacent to legitimate conservative epistemic judgment. A `to-fe-li` panel adjudicating a deflation charge must distinguish three positions that look identical from the outside: (1) genuine conservative judgment — "I don't think this has reached to-su" — not a violation; (2) `to-fe-ki` — honest misjudgment of the threshold; (3) `to-fe-ka` deflation — knowing the threshold was met, suppressing the promotion. This three-way distinction is *only resolvable objectively* if `to-fe-su` (the standards framework) defines promotion thresholds procedurally. Without `to-fe-su`, every deflation charge collapses into a subjective dispute and the `to-fe-li` role cannot adjudicate. This makes `to-fe-su` load-bearing, not optional. Register when a corpus sentence requires formal adjudication.
- [ ] **Publication-state gap.** There is currently no vocabulary distinction between a `to-fe-su` ruling that is internal (adopted by the body) and one that has been published to `to-su` status (openly systematized, challengeable by any `to-fe-li`). An unpublished standard is adoption without certification; institutional capture shows up precisely here — a body that enforces rules it has not published to `to-su` status is exercising unchallengeable authority. The distinction may eventually need a compound or qualifier. Candidate: `to-fe-su-ki` (the inchoative — the moment a standards rule enters published `to-su` status) vs bare `to-fe-su` (the body / the unpublished ruleset). Do not register until corpus demands the distinction.
- [x] ~~**`ra` particle / `ra` root collision.**~~ → **Resolved:** instrument particle renamed from `ra` to `ro` (`ro` has no root assignment). The collision was non-transparent — instrument-marking and energy/force share no semantic relationship. Policy formalized in spec/grammar.md: **Particle–Root Overlap Policy** (particles may share a root's form only when the particle's function is semantically derived from that root). `ra` now unambiguously means energy/force everywhere it appears.
- [x] ~~**`se` pronoun / `se` root collision.**~~ → **Resolved:** 3rd person pronoun renamed `se` → `ze`. No root `ze` exists. Corpus impact was zero (pronoun had not appeared in any gloss). See spec/grammar.md pronoun table.
- [x] ~~**`wi` pronoun / `wi` root collision.**~~ → **Resolved:** group pronoun renamed `wi` → `yu`. No root `yu` exists. The rename also unblocks `wi` (will/intention root) from future adoption as a purpose-frame particle without pronoun ambiguity. See spec/grammar.md pronoun table.
- [x] ~~**Need / functional dependency — confirmed gap (S057).**~~ → **Resolved by compound: `ne-fe` (S058).** `ne-fe` (relation + boundary = dependency condition) produces a genuine state predication: `lo-zo-li  ne-fe  ma` = "the person is in a dependency-condition toward substance" = "the patient needs water." This is a state description, not a causal prediction, and correctly distinguishes from S057\'s `lo-zo-li  no-ma  go  de` (causal frame). Parsing disambiguation: `ne-fe` in predicate slot is unambiguous — `ne`-as-particle expects a following noun, not a fused root. No new primitive. Register `ne-fe` as a stable compound for dependency/requirement. See corpus S058 (T-PRM-003).
- [ ] **`wi-to` semantic range — watch item.** Current attested meaning: plan (S062). Review flagged potential broadening to: design (purposive structure without single outcome), intention-model (internal goal representation), program (sequenced purposive structure). These are not identical. If corpus uses `wi-to` for all four, the compound is overloaded; if corpus differentiates them, `wi-to` narrows to one and the others need distinct forms. Do not widen the definition entry in registry/derived.md until corpus forces it. Watch for a sentence where "design" and "plan" would need to be distinguished.
- [ ] **Transfer without loss — compound candidate (S056).** Giving-with-release (`la-[giver] ne-[recipient] lo-[object] de`) composes cleanly. Agentive transfer-without-loss (sharing/copying) does not — `de` implies the giver's possession ends; `ki+ne` reads as motion, not intentional transfer. Compound candidates: `ka-ne` (intentional-recipient) or `de-ki-be`. Lexicalize when corpus produces a copy/share sentence.
- [ ] **Proof structure corpus test — deferred.** The `go`/`du` causal pair (`go` = cause/origin, `du` = result/effect) is likely the natural mechanism for expressing logical proofs: a proof is a `to-go-du` chain where each step is a `to` (conceptual pattern) linked by `go → du` (premise causes conclusion). This may be the most structurally natural use of those roots in the language. Test when corpus reaches formal/scientific register; the interesting question is whether multi-step proofs require a new boundary mechanism or compose recursively via `ne` (relation) linking `go-du` pairs. Do not test until grammar for subordinate clauses is settled (prerequisite). The language currently expresses "X is in state Y" via juxtaposition (`lo-X  state`) or via `be`/`de` change-roots. No primitive for stable condition without invoking change or evaluation. Likely surfaces after ~100–200 corpus sentences. See P-GP-001 (copula patterns). Monitor.
- [ ] Phonetic cluster `se`/`so`/`si`: evaluate survival at normal speech speed.
- [ ] Evaluate candidate root `ce` (transformation/state change) after first corpus sentences.
- [ ] `zo` subtypes: are plant/animal/fungus distinguishable by compound, or is `zo` intentionally undifferentiated?

## Mystic / Resonance Domain

- [ ] **Mystic primitive threshold — deferred watch.** Corpus batch S072–S078 tested seven mystic-domain sentences without a new primitive. Result: 6/7 clean. Existing roots cover: field (`pa-ra`), ritual (`wi-ka-su`), ward (`fe-su`), trance (`zo-se-ki`), relic (`ti-mu`), forbidden boundary (`fe-no-ka`), vision (`se-to`). No primitive gap found yet. **Key ambiguity (partially resolved):** S075 will-as-agent vs will-as-instrument. Resolved for the *relational/bidirectional* case: S083 `la-ra-ki-li  ne-ra-ki  lo-mu` (pilot enters resonance with machine via `ne-ra-ki`) distinguishes mutual energetic coupling from directed will-acting-on-target. Residual watch: the *unidirectional* will-effect case (will causes change in an external system without physical action) — if that appears repeatedly in corpus and `ro-wi` (will-as-instrument) does not satisfy it, escalate to primitive pressure. Primitive threshold unchanged: three corpus sentences with wrong readings unfixable by compound.
- [ ] **`ne-ra` (resonance) — key compound watch.** Confirmed clean in S083. `ne-ra` = relation-energy = energetic coupling between two systems. `ne-ra-ki` = to attune (inchoative). Candidate for W-registration once second corpus attestation appears. If the mecha resonance concept requires this compound in five or more sentences spanning different contexts, reconsider whether `ne-ra` is itself doing primitive-level work and whether the relational-energy concept deserves a shortform root. Do not preempt; collect evidence.
- [ ] **`zo-si` vs `zo-to` disambiguation — keep distinct.** `zo-si` = living-signal = spirit / disembodied agent (an entity that exists as signal without physical substrate). `zo-to` = living-pattern = soul / identity-pattern of a living being (the organizing conceptual structure constituting personal identity). S084 used `zo-to` for the soul-as-claim inside an epistemic frame; S085 used `zo-si` as a non-physical agent. Both worked cleanly. **Do not collapse into one compound.** The distinction is the same as `zo-si`: entity without body vs `zo-to`: organizing pattern of a body-having entity. If Concordian speakers repeatedly need both concepts in adjacent sentences, consider registering both. Current status: unregistered candidates.
- [ ] **Magic-primitive trap — principle log.** Design principle extracted from session: premature mystical primitives (mana, spirit, soul, divine) cause three failure modes: (1) the concept later proves to be a subtype of an existing primitive (mana ≈ `ra`); (2) the single primitive collapses multiple distinct concepts that later need separating; (3) the worldbuilding changes and the ontology becomes inconsistent. Tonesu avoids all three by treating mystical/religious constructs as compounds, domains, and cultural registers. **The language encodes no metaphysical commitment about whether souls and spirits are real.** A sentence like `la-yo  si  lo-mu  zo-to` asserts a doctrine claim; it does not assert the ontological reality of `zo-to`. The language is metaphysically neutral by design. Primitive threshold for any metaphysical root: corpus pressure + ontological necessity (the setting requires distinguishing the mystic substrate from `ra`, `wi`, `to`, and `ne` simultaneously, with no compound path that resolves the distinction).

## Word Formation

- [x] ~~**Decide separator convention at compound boundaries.**~~ → **Resolved: no separator — solid spelling.** Canonical written form is solid: `tofesuki` not `to-fe-su-ki`. Hyphens throughout the spec and corpus are analytical notation only (morpheme-boundary glossing, not orthography). `'` remains the only normative non-alphabetic character in a compound. Hyphens are **reserved** as a potential future structural marker for non-nominal constructions, analogous to how `'` earned its role — but that use case is not yet defined. Do not use hyphens in nominal written forms. See spec/word-formation.md § Written Form.
- [ ] Define maximum compound length before compression is required.
- [ ] Confirm suffix order when multiple derivational markers stack.
- [x] ~~**`-to`/`to-`, `-li`/`li`, `-se`/`se` suffix–root collisions.**~~ → **`-se` resolved: renamed to `-ge`.** `-ge` has no root collision (no primitive, particle, or pronoun `ge`). Design principle formalized in spec/morphology.md: a suffix must not share form with a root whose semantic domain overlaps with the suffix's derivational role. `-to`/`to-` and `-li`/`li` remain under watch but are not renamed: both pass the principle (their root and suffix functions are semantically distinct despite formal identity), and corpus tests showed `-li` mostly converges and `-to` is positionally disambiguated. The hyphen-boundary orthographic convention is **not adopted** — the problem was a bad overlap, not an orthography gap. Corpus impact: `ra-se` (S049A/C) → `ra-ge`; all future quality-suffix forms use `-ge`. See spec/morphology.md § Derivational Suffixes. S047–S055 (T-SFX-001 through T-SFX-009) tested all three pairs. Results: (1) **`-to`/`to`:** diverge — suffix = "concept-of [thing]" (final nominalizer); root as head = "doctrine/framework about [domain]". Ambiguous at right edge of a multi-root compound in nominal position. (2) **`-li`/`li`:** converge for role contexts; diverge when personhood/being-status is being asserted (e.g. AI + person-status = `to-mu-li` as root compound vs `tomu-li` as "one who operates conceptual devices"). (3) **`-se`/`se`:** diverge — quality predicate vs perception-compound; `-se` suffix flagged unstable in morphology.md; S049C confirms the instability experimentally. `ra-se` in agent position = genuine ambiguity between "energetic entity" and "energy sensor." **Shared resolution across all three:** hyphen-boundary notation — `X-Y-Z` (compound, final element is a root) vs `XY-Z` (root chain + suffix, final element is a pure suffix with no hyphen before it). This distinguishes them in writing without changing spoken forms. **`-se` escalation:** morphology.md already flags `-se` as requiring "justification or rename before stable." S049C makes this urgent — the `-se`/`se` pair generates the sharpest collision. Targeted resolution for `-se` before the other two. Do not close until hyphen-boundary convention is written into word-formation.md and morphology.md's `-se` flag is resolved.
- [ ] **X-X repetition rule — do not formalize yet; monitor corpus.** S040–S044 confirm that repeating a primitive (X-X) produces a stable meta-level reading via ordinary head-final compounding. No new mechanism needed. Five pairs tested: `to-to` (meta-concept), `su-su` (meta-structure/architecture), `ka-ka` (action-governance/procedure), `se-se` (sensor self-monitoring/introspection), `ne-ne` (topology). All parse cleanly. However: (1) the `-to` suffix / `to-` compound collision must be resolved first (above); (2) a second round of corpus sentences should test X-X compounds against X-Y alternatives in the same domain to confirm the meta-level reading is the *only* valid parse (not just one plausible parse among others). Formalize in word-formation.md once both conditions are met.
- [x] ~~**Compound grouping marker `'` — ready to adopt into word-formation.md.**~~ → **Resolved.** Full rule written into spec/word-formation.md § Compound Grouping Marker. Phonological status: prosodic juncture (spec/phonology.md). Directionality: left boundary of subcompound. Usage policy: omit for 2–3 roots; optional at depth-4; expected with X-X in longer chains; one `'` per compound maximum — restructure as phrase if a second would be needed. Corpus basis: S045 (T-APO-001), S046 (T-APO-002).
