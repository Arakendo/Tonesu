# Translation Test: Star Wars — The Jedi Code and the Sith Code

## Source: George Lucas / Wookieepedia canonical texts
## Status: Draft — first pass

---

## Purpose

The Jedi and Sith Codes are among the most cited short texts in popular fiction. Structurally they are an antithetical pair: the Sith Code explicitly inverts the opening claim of the Jedi Code. Together they test a cluster of Tonesu constructions that rarely appear in theological or scientific translation but are central to this kind of philosophical-practical declaration.

Primary tests:

- **Bi-clausal parallel `/`** — the Jedi Code is five lines, each of the form "there is no X / there is Y." This is the `/` construction under maximum philosophical load: not just poetic juxtaposition (Bashō BSH-001) but explicit ontological denial + affirmation.
- **Scope-derived `ara` as canonical rendering of the Force** — `a-` (abstract/universal) + `ra` (energy/force) = universal-abstract-energy. No new compound needed; the scope system produces the right reading compositionally.
- **`ru-fe` exclusive scope particle** — Sith line 1: "there is ONLY passion."
- **`to-fe-ka` (W029) as "is a lie"** — deliberate epistemic mislabeling, which is stronger than mere falsehood; the Sith's claim is that peace is an actively maintained deception.
- **`go {…},` causal chain** — the four "through X I gain Y" lines of the Sith Code use `go` (necessary causal mechanism) rather than `;` (mere sequence). This encodes the Sith claim as causal ontology, not a casual correlation.
- **Agency shift at lines 5–6 of the Sith Code** — `la-mi` (I as agent) throughout lines 2–4, then `lo-mi` (I as patient) in lines 5–6: the speaker's chains break *due to the causal structure* (`go {wi-du}`), and `la-ara` (the Force as agent) liberates `lo-mi`. The Sith's self-empowerment narrative ends with the Force as the true liberating agent.

Secondary tests:

- New affect compounds: `fa-wi-ra` (passion), `fa-su` (serenity) — extending the `fa-[X]` family
- Social-relational peace: `ne-no-ra` (relation-without-force) vs bare affect-calm
- Victory as will-result: `wi-du`
- Death as organism-decrease: `de-zo` (first explicit registration; referenced in W128 notes)

Corpus sentences: **S588–S598**

| `ne-no-ra` | peace | W171 · `ne` (relation) + `no` (negation) + `ra` (force) = relation-without-force | Peace as a relational property: a bond that lacks coercive force. Not the absence of all affect, but the presence of connection that carries no violence |

### `ara` — scope derivation, not a compound

`ara` is not registered as a W-entry because it follows productively from the `a-` scope-modifier prefix applied to the primitive `ra`. The `a-` prefix means "abstract / universal / most general reading." `a-ra` = energy in its most expansive sense: not tied to any particular force, field, or instance.

This matches the canonical Star Wars description of the Force: "an energy field created by all living things" that "surrounds us, penetrates us, binds the galaxy together." The Force is not a specific energy but the universal substrate of energetic being — exactly `a-ra`.

First explicit use in corpus: S592 and S598. These are also the first sentences to attest `la-ara` (the Force as grammatical agent).

### `ne-no-ra` vs `fa-su` — two distinct readings of peace/serenity

The Jedi Code uses different words for "peace" (line 1) and "serenity" (line 3):

- **`ne-no-ra`** (peace, S588): a *relational* property — a bond that lacks coercive force. Peace is not isolation or affect-absence; it is the presence of connection without violence. The Jedi Code's line 1 says: replace raw affect with the quality of non-violent connection.
- **`fa-su`** (serenity, S590): an *affective* property — the substrate in its ordered form. Serenity is not the absence of affect but its structuring. Line 3 says: replace driven/forceful affect with affect that has found structure.

These are complementary: `ne-no-ra` = peace as a social/relational fact; `fa-su` = serenity as an inward/affective fact.

### The `fa-wi-ra` (passion) / `fa-su` (serenity) contrast

`fa-wi-ra` = affect-will-force: affect that has been given a direction (will) and a driving character (force). This is passion as *compulsion* — the affect that becomes a purposeful engine. The Sith Code celebrates it; the Jedi Code rejects it.

`fa-su` = affect-structure: affect that has been given form without being given force or direction. Serenity is what passion would be if it stopped driving.

### The causal chain (S594–S596) — `go` vs `;`

The Sith Code's "through X I gain Y" lines use `go {…},` (causal mechanism), not `;` (sequential connection). This is a deliberate choice. `;` would only say "passion happened, and then strength followed" — mere constant conjunction. `go` says "passion is the necessary condition that produces strength" — Hume's necessary connection. The Sith are not describing a statistical correlation; they are asserting a causal ontology of self-empowerment.

## Source Texts

### The Jedi Code

> There is no emotion, there is peace.
> There is no ignorance, there is knowledge.
> There is no passion, there is serenity.
> There is no chaos, there is harmony.
> There is no death, there is the Force.

*Note: this is the "classical" or "formal" version, attributed to Odan-Urr. The older "mantra" version ("Emotion, yet peace...") inverts the structure to affirmation-then-acknowledgment. This batch translates the classical version as it is the stronger philosophical statement.*

### The Sith Code

> Peace is a lie, there is only passion.
> Through passion, I gain strength.
> Through strength, I gain power.
> Through power, I gain victory.
> Through victory, my chains are broken.
> The Force shall free me.

*Source: Rule of Two canon (Darth Bane era). The Sith Code directly inverts the Jedi Code's first claim and replaces the Jedi's static affirmations with a causal progression.*

## Translation Analysis

### S588 — Jedi Code, line 1 — "There is no emotion, there is peace."

```
no-fa  ne  /  ne-no-ra  ne
```

**Written:** `nofa ne / nenora ne`

**Natural reading:** No raw affect is / peaceful-relation is.

**Notes:** The `/` bi-clausal parallel pairs two stative assertions. `no-fa` = absence/negation of the affective substrate = no raw reactive affect. `ne-no-ra` = relation-without-force = peaceful bond/connection (W171, new). First attestation of `ne-no-ra`.

The structural revelation: `ne-no-ra` is not merely the absence of affect. It is the presence of a specific kind of bond — one that carries no force or violence. The Jedi Code doesn't say "be empty of affect"; it says "let the quality of your connections be force-free." Peace is a relational property here, not a personal one.

---

### S589 — Jedi Code, line 2 — "There is no ignorance, there is knowledge."

```
no-to  ne  /  to  ne
```

**Written:** `noto ne / to ne`

**Natural reading:** No knowledge-absence is / knowledge is.

**Notes:** The cleanest line of the code. `no-to` = negation-of-conceptual-pattern = absence of thought/model = ignorance. `to` = knowledge/thought (primitive). The parallel is tautological in the best sense: `no-no-knowledge / knowledge` — the Jedi Code asserts a totality-claim about the epistemic domain. Nothing is proposed; knowledge simply prevails. No new vocabulary; `to` is the primitive.

---

### S590 — Jedi Code, line 3 — "There is no passion, there is serenity."

```
no-fa-wi-ra  ne  /  fa-su  ne
```

**Written:** `nofawira ne / fasu ne`

**Natural reading:** No driven-forceful-affect is / structured-affect is.

**Notes:** First attestations of `fa-wi-ra` (W172, passion) and `fa-su` (W173, serenity). The negation reveals what passion IS: affect that has been given a will-direction and forced character (`fa-wi-ra`). Serenity is the alternative state: affect with structure but without drive or force (`fa-su`).

The `fa-[X]` family now includes: `fa-ki` (W093, affect shifting), `fa-de` (W094, affect fading), `fa-no` (W095, affect inactive), `fa-re` (W096, affect cycling), `fa-ra` (W154, fear/awe), `fa-ra-be` (W123, anger), `fa-wi-de` (W127, temptation), `fa-wi-zi` (W136, lust), `fa-wi-ra` (W172, passion), `fa-su` (W173, serenity).

---

### S591 — Jedi Code, line 4 — "There is no chaos, there is harmony."

```
no-su  ne  /  su-ne  ne
```

**Written:** `nosu ne / sune ne`

**Natural reading:** No structure is / structure-of-connection is.

**Notes:** `no-su` = absence of structure = chaos. `su-ne` = structure-of-relation = harmony (W174, new). The parallel reveals the definition: chaos is `no-su` (the complete absence of structure), and harmony is `su-ne` (the specific structural arrangement in which relations are properly ordered). Harmony is not just "structure" — it is the particular structural mode where connections are well-arranged. First attestation of `su-ne`.

---

### S592 — Jedi Code, line 5 — "There is no death, there is the Force."

```
no-de-zo  ne  /  ara  ne
```

**Written:** `nodezo ne / ara ne`

**Natural reading:** No organism-decrease is / universal-energy is.

**Notes:** First explicit registration of `de-zo` (W178, death as organism-decrease) and first corpus attestation of `ara` (universal energy = the Force). The line states the Jedi cosmology: when death (`de-zo`) is negated, what prevails is `ara` — the abstract-universal energetic field. Death is not replaced by life (`zo-ra`, W117) but by the encompassing force of which life is one expression.

`ara` = `a-` + `ra`: the scope-prefix `a-` raises `ra` (energy/force) to its universal, unbounded reading. This is the correct Tonesu rendering of the Force: not a specific energy but the universal substrate. `ara ne` = "universal-energy is / prevails." The Jedi Code ends with the one assertion that requires no negation: `ara` simply is.

---

### S593 — Sith Code, line 1 — "Peace is a lie, there is only passion."

```
ne-no-ra  ne  to-fe-ka  /  ru-fe,  fa-wi-ra  ne
```

**Written:** `nenora ne tofeka / rufe, fawira ne`

**Natural reading:** Peaceful-relation is deliberate-mislabeling / only: driven-affect is.

**Notes:** The `/` parallel again, but now the first clause is an accusation: `ne-no-ra ne to-fe-ka` = peace (W171) IS epistemic-mislabeling-deliberate (W029, `to-fe-ka`). The Sith don't say "peace doesn't exist" (`ne-no-ra ne no`) — they say it is an *intentional fabrication*. `to-fe-ka` requires an agent who performs the mislabeling; the Sith claim peace is not merely absent but actively maintained as a deception.

Second clause: `ru-fe, fa-wi-ra ne` — the exclusive scope particle `ru-fe` makes the claim: only passion (`fa-wi-ra`, W172) prevails. First use of `ru-fe` in combination with a new `fa-[X]` compound.

This sentence also performs the direct structural inversion of S588: both sentences contain `ne-no-ra ne`, but S588 establishes it as true (`no-fa ne / ne-no-ra ne`) and S593 attributes it as false (`ne-no-ra ne to-fe-ka`). The two codes are grammatically locked: the same three words (`ne-no-ra ne`) take opposite truth-status assignments.

---

### S594 — Sith Code, line 2 — "Through passion, I gain strength."

```
go {fa-wi-ra},  la-mi  ra-be
```

**Written:** `go {fawira}, lami rabe`

**Natural reading:** [Cause: passion] → I grow in force.

**Notes:** First link in the Sith causal chain. `go {fa-wi-ra}` = by means of passion (causal mechanism, necessary connection — not mere sequence; `;` would be wrong here). `la-mi ra-be` = I grow in force/energy = I gain strength. `ra-be` = `ra` (force/energy) + `be` (increasing) = force-growing, composable. Strength is `ra` in its increasing form — raw energetic capacity building.

Note: `go {...},` with braces is the structural slot notation for the causal premise. The claim is that passion is the *necessary condition* for strength's emergence — this is an ontological claim about what produces what, not a description of coincidence.

---

### S595 — Sith Code, line 3 — "Through strength, I gain power."

```
go {ra},  la-mi  wi-ra-be
```

**Written:** `go {ra}, lami wirabe`

**Natural reading:** [Cause: force] → I grow in directed-power.

**Notes:** Second link. `go {ra}` = by means of force/strength. `la-mi wi-ra-be` = I grow in directed-power = I gain authority/power. `wi-ra-be` = `wi-ra` (W177, will-force = directed power) + `be` (increasing). The escalation is visible in the compounds: `ra` (raw force) → `wi-ra` (force made purposeful). Strength is generic energetic capacity; power is strength organized by intent. The `wi` component is what distinguishes power from mere strength.

---

### S596 — Sith Code, line 4 — "Through power, I gain victory."

```
go {wi-ra},  la-mi  wi-du-be
```

**Written:** `go {wira}, lami widube`

**Natural reading:** [Cause: directed-power] → I grow in [willed]-achievement.

**Notes:** Third link. `go {wi-ra}` = by means of directed power. `la-mi wi-du-be` = I grow in will-result = I gain victory. `wi-du-be` = `wi-du` (W175, victory/achievement = will-result) + `be` (increasing). Victory is the result of directed power: `wi-ra` (force made purposeful) → `wi-du` (the outcome that purpose achieves). The chain has moved from raw energy (S594) to directed energy (S595) to achieved outcome (S596). All three steps have `la-mi` as explicit agent.

---

### S597 — Sith Code, line 5 — "Through victory, my chains are broken."

```
go {wi-du},  lo-mi  ko-ne-mu  de-ki
```

**Written:** `go {widu}, lomi konemu deki`

**Natural reading:** [Cause: victory] → my chains enter breaking.

**Notes:** The agency shift. `la-mi` disappears; `lo-mi` appears as patient/beneficiary. `lo-mi ko-ne-mu` = the chains that bind me / my chains. `de-ki` = decrease-enter = enter the state of breaking/dissolution. First attestations of `ko-ne-mu` (W176, chain/shackle = containment-connection-artifact).

The agent of "my chains break" is absent: the chains break *as a consequence of the causal structure* (`go {wi-du}`), not as a deliberate act by the speaker. This is a significant grammatical point: the Sith does not break their own chains — victory causes the chains to break. The speaker is now `lo-mi` (the one affected), not `la-mi` (the one acting).

---

### S598 — Sith Code, line 6 — "The Force shall free me."

```
la-ara  lo-mi  ka-pa-ki
```

**Written:** `laara lomi kapaki`

**Natural reading:** The Force acts-to-liberate me.

**Notes:** `la-ara` = the Force (universal-energy, `ara`) as agent (`la-`). `lo-mi` = me as patient. `ka-pa-ki` = liberation act (deliberate action that produces place-motion = movement out of containment; W167 context: from EXO-001 `ka-pa-ki` root). First attestation of `ara` as a grammatical agent.

The final sentence completes the agency inversion: throughout S594–S596, `la-mi` (the Sith) was the agent of all verbs. In S597–S598, the speaker is `lo-mi` — the one acted upon. In S598, `la-ara` is the explicit agent: the Force itself frees the speaker. The Sith Code's narrative of self-empowerment resolves into a sentence where the speaker is passive and the Force is active. Whether this is the intended meaning is a design-space question that Tonesu's grammar makes unavoidable to consider.

Phonological note: `la-ara` → written `laara`. The `la-` prefix ends in `a`; `ara` begins with `a-` (scope prefix). The written form `laara` is valid: both syllables begin with consonants (`l-`, `r-`), satisfying Parse Invariant 1. The double `a` is a surface coincidence of the prefix stack.

### Cross-code findings

**Finding 1: `ara` = the Force.** The scope derivation `a-` + `ra` (universal + energy/force) lands precisely on the canonical description of the Force. No new primitive needed; no new compound needed. The scope system produces this compositionally. First attestation S592; first as grammatical agent S598.

**Finding 2: The bi-clausal parallel `/` carries the Jedi Code's full philosophical weight.** All five Jedi lines are structurally `no-X ne / Y ne` — existential denial paired with existential affirmation. The parallel construction maps perfectly onto the theological-practical declaration format.

**Finding 3: `ne-no-ra ne` appears in both codes with opposite truth-values.** S588 establishes it (`no-fa ne / ne-no-ra ne` = the Jedi affirmation). S593 denies it (`ne-no-ra ne to-fe-ka` = the Sith accusation). The grammatical overlap is deliberate: the two codes are locked together by this phrase.

**Finding 4: The Sith Code's causal chain (`go {X}, la-mi Y-be`) requires `go`, not `;`.** The Sith assert necessary causal connection — ontological production — not correlation. `;` would reduce the claim to coincidence. The batch confirms that `go` is the correct particle here.

**Finding 5: The Sith Code ends with the Force as agent.** S597–S598 shift agent from `la-mi` to `lo-mi` (then `la-ara`). The self-empowerment narrative concludes with the speaker as patient. Tonesu's explicit agent-marking makes this visible in a way the English does not.
