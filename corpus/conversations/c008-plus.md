## `ze` Anaphora Stress Test (C008)

---

**C008 — Arbitration hearing: `ze` under competing person and proposition referents**

*Scene: A `to-fe-li` (epistemic arbiter, role B) is conducting a compliance hearing. A
`ra-ki-li` (pilot, role A) is testifying that a `to-su-li` (archivist, absent third party,
referred to as `ze`) suppressed a signal record. The arbiter questions A's testimony. The
exchange manufactures four distinct `lo-ze` uses: (1) person-reading, unambiguous agent;
(2) propositional-reading, forced by predicate-type incompatibility; (3) genuinely
ambiguous — `to-fe-ka` predicate applies coherently to both persons and propositions;
(4) disambiguation by same-turn restatement. Resolution of P-PR-001.*

*Tests: `ze` as person vs propositional back-reference; predicate-type disambiguation;
most-recent-salient rule; restatement as the canonical disambiguation strategy; `no`
contrast coordinator as disambiguation tool.*

---

**Turn A1 — Person-reading (`la-ze` agent slot)**

```
Gloss:    la-ze  de  lo-si-de  ta-ti-de
Literal:  agent:ze  decayed  patient:record-of-signal  at-past-time
Natural:  The archivist suppressed the record.
          (literally: caused the past-signal record to decay)
```

**Notes:**
- `la-ze` = archivist (absent third party). Agent slot; unambiguous person-reading.
- `de lo-si-de` = caused the past-signal record to decay = suppressed/destroyed the
  document. `si-de` (W098: past-signal, signal-of-record) in the patient slot.
  `ta-ti-de` = at past time.
- **State of discourse after A1:** ze-the-person is the sole salient referent. No
  proposition is yet a discourse entity.

---

**Turn B1 — Competing proposition enters discourse**

```
Gloss:    la-mi  si  [la-ze  no-de  lo-si-de]
Literal:  agent:I  hypothesize  [agent:ze  not-decayed  patient:record-of-signal]
Natural:  I hypothesize: ze did not suppress the record.
```

**Notes:**
- B challenges A1 at `si` level — not asserting the counterclaim as established, only
  as hypothesis. Correct Tonesu practice: challenge at the lowest confident epistemic
  level.
- **State of discourse after B1:** two referents are now simultaneously salient:
  (1) ze-the-person (from A1, `la-ze` agent of the alleged act); and
  (2) B1's counterclaim proposition (`la-ze  no-de  lo-si-de`) as an explicit discourse
  entity. This is the collision that P-PR-001 anticipates.

---

**Turn A2 — Propositional `ze`, forced by predicate-type**

```
Gloss:    la-mi  to  [lo-ze  se]
Literal:  agent:I  established  [patient:that-claim  raw-perception]
Natural:  I hold as established: that claim is mere raw perception.
```

**Notes:**
- `lo-ze` = B1's proposition — not ze-the-person. A is dismissing the counterclaim as
  being at `se` level: B's hypothesis lacks evidential grounding.
- **Why person-reading is blocked:** `se` (raw perception / signal-level epistemic datum)
  predicates of data, claims, and epistemic states — not of persons. `lo-ze  se` read as
  "ze (the archivist) is raw perception" is incoherent: persons are not epistemic data
  levels. Therefore `ze` is forced to the propositional referent (B1's claim).
- **Predicate-type disambiguation confirmed.** Same form as C005 B1 (`la-mi  to  [lo-ze
  se]` — identical) where it was also propositional and also unambiguous. A2 adds the
  crucial analytical dimension: the person-referent was actively in discourse and *still*
  the proposition was forced by predicate incompatibility.
- A's escalation pattern: A opens with a `to`-level denial of B's epistemic status,
  not a re-assertion of the underlying charge. Methodical: address the challenge before
  re-asserting the original claim.

---

**Turn B2 — Genuinely ambiguous `lo-ze`**

```
Gloss:    la-mi  to  [lo-ze  to-fe-ka]
Literal:  agent:I  established  [patient:ze  deliberate-fraud]
Natural:  I hold as established: [ze / that claim] is deliberate fraud.
```

**Notes:**
- **First genuine `ze` ambiguity in the corpus.** Two coherent readings:
  - **Person-reading:** "I hold as established that ze (the archivist) committed
    deliberate epistemic boundary violation." B is counter-charging ze — the same entity
    A accused in A1.
  - **Proposition-reading:** "I hold as established that A2's move — dismissing my
    testimony as mere `se` — is itself `to-fe-ka`." B is accusing A of epistemic
    deflation: deliberately downgrading B's evidenced hypothesis to `se` level.
- `to-fe-ka` (W029: deliberate epistemic boundary violation) applies coherently to both
  persons (as agents of the violation) and to propositions/claims (as acts or moves that
  constitute the violation). No predicate-type incompatibility is possible here.
- **Neither referent is more grammatically prominent.** B1 introduced a proposition;
  A1 introduced the person. Both are one turn away from B2. The discourse does not
  structurally favour either.
- This is the breaking point P-PR-001 was watching for.

---

**Turn A3 — Disambiguation by restatement**

```
Gloss:    la-ze  to-fe-ka  no  la-mi
Literal:  agent:ze  deliberate-fraud  [contrast]  agent:I
Natural:  Ze committed the fraud — not me.
```

**Notes:**
- **Disambiguation by NP restatement in the same sentence.** A interprets B2 as the
  person-reading (ze charged with fraud) and clarifies: `la-ze ... no la-mi` uses the
  `no` contrast coordinator to make explicit who is the agent of `to-fe-ka`. "Ze did it,
  not me." This rules out the proposition-reading entirely — if B had been charging A's
  epistemic move (A2) as fraudulent, the contrast referent would not be `la-mi`; A is
  instead re-asserting ze-the-person as the fraud actor.
- **`no` contrast coordinator as disambiguation tool.** The standard Level 4 negation
  (`[actual constituent]  no  [rejected alternative]`, formalized spec/grammar.md §
  Negation) performs double duty here: it clarifies the referent AND asserts the charge.
  `la-ze  to-fe-ka  no  la-mi` = "[It is] ze [who committed] fraud, not me."
- **Most-recently-salient rule confirmed by failure mode.** A could not rely on recency
  alone; `to-fe-ka` was too compatible with both referents. Restatement was *required*
  to resolve the ambiguity. This is the finding: the most-recent-salient rule is
  insufficient when a predicate applies symmetrically to both types.

---

**Turn B3 — Arbiter's record: explicit NP, no ze back-reference**

```
Gloss:    la-mi  se  [la-ze  de  lo-si-de]  —  la-tu  ka-si  lo-to-fe-su
Literal:  agent:I  perceive  [agent:ze  decayed  patient:record-of-signal]  —
          agent:you  action:signal  patient:epistemic-standards-framework
Natural:  I note (perceptually): ze suppressed a record. — Submit this to the standards body.
```

**Notes:**
- **First positive attestation of `la-mi  se  [embedded clause]`.** The epistemic modal
  frame `la-[speaker]  se  [proposition]` had its corpus column in spec/grammar.md marked
  "positive awaited" — the negated form `no-se` was attested at C007 B4 but the positive
  `la-mi  se  [prop]` had not appeared in the record until now. C008 B3 closes that gap.
  The positive form means: "I have perceptual / signal-level grounds for noting [prop]."
  Applied to testimony: the arbiter received A's claim as a perceptual input (they heard
  it) and records it at exactly that level — no higher.
- **Arbiter uses `se` (perceptual/floor level) for the ruling-note.** B does not certify
  A's charge at `to` or `si` level — B acknowledges A's testimony as perceptually received
  (`se`) and routes it to `to-fe-su` for adjudication. Correct Tonesu arbitration
  form: the arbiter records the testimony at the level it was received, not at a higher
  asserted level.
- **Explicit NP `la-ze` replaces `lo-ze` in B3.** The arbiter re-states A1's claim in
  full (`la-ze  de  lo-si-de`) rather than using `ze` back-reference. This is the
  Tonesu formal-register practice: once ambiguity has been produced in a proceeding,
  explicit NPs are preferred over pronouns in the official record. The rationale is
  institutional, not grammatical: the record must be unambiguous to `to-fe-su`.
- `la-tu  ka-si  lo-to-fe-su` = second corpus use of `to-fe-su` routing (first: C005 B3).
  Same institutional routing pattern; confirms it is productive.

---

**C008 Gaps and pressures:**

| Gap | Location | Resolution |
|-----|----------|------------|
| **Genuine `ze` ambiguity** | B2 | First confirmed instance. Neither predicate-type constraint nor recency resolves it. Disambiguation required in following turn. |
| **`to-fe-ka` predicate directionality** | B2, A3 | B2's ambiguity includes *who* is charged — person ze or A's epistemic move. The predicate itself carries no directionality marker. |
| **Formal register NP preference** | B3 | Arbiter avoids `ze` in the record after ambiguity occurred. Is this a Tonesu convention or a speaker-preference? Unclear from one case. |

---

**Verdict C008: `ze` anaphora rule formalized. P-PR-001 resolved.**

Four `lo-ze` / `la-ze` uses across six turns, producing three distinct resolution outcomes:

| Turn | `ze` referent | Resolution mechanism |
|------|--------------|---------------------|
| A1 | Person (forced) | Agent-slot person-referent; no competing proposition |
| A2 | Proposition (forced) | Predicate-type: `se` is incompatible with persons |
| B2 | **Ambiguous** | `to-fe-ka` compatible with both; no structural resolution |
| A3 | Person (disambiguated) | Same-turn NP restatement: `la-ze ... no la-mi` |

**Three-mechanism resolution rule:**

1. **Predicate-type incompatibility (Mechanism 1).** When the predicate applies only to
   one referent type (epistemic qualities → propositions; motion/location → entities),
   `ze` is forced to the compatible type. No ambiguity produced; no repair needed.

2. **Most-recent-salient (Mechanism 2).** When the predicate is compatible with both
   types, `ze` defaults to the most recently active discourse entity. "Most active" tracks
   which entity was most recently introduced or modified in the conversational record.
   This mechanism is *insufficient* when both referents are equidistant in recency
   and the predicate is symmetric — see B2.

3. **Restatement (Mechanism 3).** When Mechanisms 1 and 2 both fail, Tonesu practice
   is to restate with an explicit NP in the following turn. `ze` is not disambiguated
   within the ambiguous turn; the repair happens one turn later. This is not a grammar
   failure — it is expected. The language produces the ambiguity; the discourse resolves it.

**Design decision: `ze` is a unified anaphora, not a split class.**
`ze` does not have separate person-`ze` and proposition-`ze` forms. It picks the most
salient discourse entity of any type, subject to predicate-type compatibility. Tonesu
does not require a dedicated propositional pronoun (comparable to English "that" / "which"
in "I challenge that claim" vs "I challenge her") because the disambiguation mechanisms
above handle all productive cases. Adding a split would add phonological inventory for
a problem that resolves by discourse.

**P-PR-001 closed.** (March 2026, C008)
