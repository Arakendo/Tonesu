    C001 · First conversation: backup unit failure

    The relay platform, shortly after S027. Engineer A (ze) has discovered the backup power unit is also failing. Engineer B (a colleague) arrives.

    A1 la-mi pa-re-mu lo-de ka-se-past I found damage in the backup unit.

    B1 de vo to-si? What kind of damage?

    A2 lo-re-mu de-now ti-fe ki-now The backup unit is decaying.
    [It] approaches the time-limit.

    B2 ta-now fe-ki? Does it cross the limit today?

    A3 la-mi si [fe-ki-now] ne-li-su-li ka-fe-si I assess [as signal-level]: it crosses the limit now.
    Warn the coordinator.

    B3 ka-to-su-ki ka-ki-now Understood.
    I'm going now. / On my way.

    C002 · Technical coordination: relay failure

    Two engineers on different platforms. A is live at the relay installation; B is directing remotely.

    A1 lo-si-mu no-ru The relay is unstable.

    B1 ka-se lo-si-su-mu Examine the antenna array. [Imperative — la-tu dropped]

    A2 lo-ne-mu de The connector is broken.

    B2 ka-de-be lo-ne-mu Repair the connector. [Imperative]

    A3 lo-si-mu ru ta-now The relay is stable now.

    C003 · Casual social: travel question

    Two non-engineers in everyday context. A asks about travel plans.

    A1 to-si — la-tu ki pa-li-pu ta-ti-be Are you going to the city soon?

    B1 la-mi ki pa-li-pu ta-ti-be I'm going to the city soon.

    A2 lo-ma-su be-now pa-ze A new water facility is there now.

    B2 la-mi wi [ka-se lo-ze] I intend to go see it.

    C004 · Disagreement: disputed damage assessment

    Two engineers have both examined the same unit (following C002 pattern). They disagree on its status.

    A1 lo-mu de The unit is damaged.

    B1 la-mi si [lo-mu be-now] I assess: the machine is still operating.

    A2 la-se-ka be [lo-mu de] The examination produces the finding: the machine has decayed.

    B2 lo-de no-fe — lo-mu be-now The decay has not reached the limit. The machine still functions.

    A3 la-tu ka-si lo-li-su-li lo-ze Signal the coordinator about it. [Imperative]

    C005 · Theological dispute: doctrine epistemic status

    A ra-ki-li (pilot) and a to-su-li (doctrine-keeper/archivist) dispute whether the community's guardian machine has zo-to (soul/identity-pattern). The pilot recently experienced ne-ra-ki (attunement) with the machine (S083-type event). The community's to-re-su makes the doctrinal claim. The dispute will escalate from personal assertion through doctrinal citation, to to-fe-ka charge, to institutional routing.

    A1 la-mi si [lo-mu zo-to] I hold as hypothesis: the machine has a soul.

    B1 la-mi to [lo-ze se] I hold as established: what you have (that experience) is raw perception.

    A2 la-to-re-su ko [lo-mu zo-to] The canonical doctrine holds: the machine has a soul.

    B2 la-mi to [lo-to-re-su no-to-fe ta-ti-now] I hold as established: the canonical doctrine has not crossed the epistemic
    threshold — not to this day.

    A3 la-mi to [lo-ze to-fe-ka] I hold as established: what you just said is deliberate epistemic fraud.

    B3 la-mi no-to [lo-ze to-fe-ka] — la-tu ka-si lo-to-fe-su I hold as not-established: that is not fraud. — Submit this to the standards body.

    C006 · Pre-jump briefing: polar question, content question, and the ambiguous case

    Navigator A (di-ki-li) and Pilot B (ra-ki-li) in a pre-jump sequence. The field (pa-ra) ahead may carry resonance conditions. Six turns covering all four question-and-answer pressures from the open-question log.

    A1 to-si — lo-pa-ra be-now Is the field active?

    B1 ru — lo-pa-ra be-now ra-vo nu-be lo-si-fe Yes — the field is active and above threshold.

    A2 lo-pa-ra ne-ra vo to-si? What is the character of the field's resonance?

    B2 lo-ne-ra no-to-fe — lo-pa-ra no-ne-fe lo-mu The resonance character is uncertified. The field has no link to the machine.

    A3 to-si — la-mi ne-ra-ki [Am I attuning? / What am I attuning to?]

    B3 no — lo-pa-ra no-ne-fe ta-ti-now No — the field has no link right now.

    A4 la-mi si [ta-ti-be ne-ra-ki be] I assess: the link will develop soon.

    B4 ru — ta-ti-re ki Yes — it's coming up in the cycle.

    C007 · Formal testimony: the negated epistemic scale

    After the C006 field anomaly, the ship's knowledge-keeper (to-fe-ki-li, A) conducts a formal post-event testimony session with pilot B (ra-ki-li) regarding what the pilot knew — and did not know — during the approach.

    A1 to-si — la-tu to [lo-pa-ra nu-be lo-si-fe] Do you hold as established: the field exceeded threshold?

    B1 ru — la-mi to [lo-pa-ra nu-be lo-si-fe] Yes — I hold as established: the field exceeded threshold.

    A2 lo-ne-ra vo to-si? What is the resonance character?

    B2 la-mi no-to [lo-ne-ra vo] I do not hold the resonance character as established.

    A3 to-si — la-tu si [lo-ne-ra vo] Do you hypothesize a resonance character?

    B3 la-mi no-si [lo-ne-ra vo] I do not hypothesize a resonance character.

    A4 to-si — la-tu se [lo-ne-ra] Do you perceive the resonance?

    B4 la-mi no-se [lo-ne-ra] I have no perceptual basis for the resonance.

    A5 la-mi to [la-tu no-se lo-ne-ra] I hold as established: you have no perceptual basis for the resonance.

    B5 ru Acknowledged.

    C008 · Arbitration hearing: ze under competing person and proposition referents

    A to-fe-li (epistemic arbiter, role B) is conducting a compliance hearing. A ra-ki-li (pilot, role A) is testifying that a to-su-li (archivist, absent third party, referred to as ze) suppressed a signal record. The arbiter questions A's testimony. The exchange manufactures four distinct lo-ze uses: (1) person-reading, unambiguous agent; (2) propositional-reading, forced by predicate-type incompatibility; (3) genuinely ambiguous — to-fe-ka predicate applies coherently to both persons and propositions; (4) disambiguation by same-turn restatement. Resolution of P-PR-001.

    A1 la-ze de lo-si-de ta-ti-de The archivist suppressed the record.
    (literally: caused the past-signal record to decay)

    B1 la-mi si [la-ze no-de lo-si-de] I hypothesize: ze did not suppress the record.

    A2 la-mi to [lo-ze se] I hold as established: that claim is mere raw perception.

    B2 la-mi to [lo-ze to-fe-ka] I hold as established: [ze / that claim] is deliberate fraud.

    A3 la-ze to-fe-ka no la-mi Ze committed the fraud — not me.

    B3 la-mi se [la-ze de lo-si-de] — la-tu ka-si lo-to-fe-su I note (perceptually): ze suppressed a record. — Submit this to the standards body.
