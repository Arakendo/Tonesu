## CDA-001 · Communications Decency Act — Section 230(c)(1)

<span id="S639"></span>
**S639**
`la-su-ka-li  lo-to-ki'ne-su  ne  no-si-go-li  lo-to-si  go  zo-li`
*A service-provider of an online platform is not a content-originator of information-signals from persons.*

<span id="S640"></span>
**S640**
`la-to-ki'ne-su  ne  si-mu  lu-zo-li  /  ne  no-si-go-li`
*An online platform is a relay device for persons / is not a content-originator.*

<span id="S641"></span>
**S641**
`go  la-su-ka-li  ka-to-fe  lo-to-si,  du  la-su-ka-li  ne  si-go-li`
*If a service-provider deliberately categorizes information, then the service-provider is a content-originator.*

<span id="S642"></span>
**S642**
`la-wi-fe  no  to-su-ki  lo-to-fe  su-ka-li  ne  si-go-li`
*The rule has not comprehended the boundary between service-provider and content-originator.*

<span id="S643"></span>
**S643**
`la-wi-fe  ne  no-su-to  /  ne  wi-fe'ka-to-fe`
*The rule is not a self-evident proposition / it is a rule about deliberate categorization.*

<span id="S644"></span>
**S644**
`la-wi-fe  ne  wi-fe'ka-to-fe  /  ke, la-wi-fe  no  to-su-ki  lo-to-fe  su-ka-li  ne  si-go-li`
*The rule is a prescription about categorization / but the rule has not defined the boundary between service-provider and content-originator.*
