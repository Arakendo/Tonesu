## SOL-001 · 

**Purpose:** First-attest W217 (`wi-ra-li`, spirit/directed-force-agent) and W218 (`ka-be-ne`, binding/deliberate bond-creation) in corpus sentences. Source: the framing narrative of the Ars Goetia (Lesser Key of Solomon). Tests: authority construction via DIP-001 `ne`+property pattern, numeral phrase in patient slot, result slot as destination (`lu-ko-mu`), instrumental `go-si`, proper-name agent and group NP, and causal nominalization with juncture (`go-ka-no-ko'ko-mu`).

<span id="S672"></span>
**S672**
`la-Solomon  ne  wi-ra  lo-yom bun nu wi-ra-li`
*Solomon held authority over 72 spirits of directed power.*

!!! annotation "Notes"
    Authority construction via DIP-001 pattern: `la-X  ne  wi-ra  lo-Y` = X holds directed-power over Y. `wi-ra` (W177) serves as the mediating property connecting agent to patient via `ne`. Numeral phrase in patient position: `lo-yom bun nu wi-ra-li` = "patient: 72 (7·10+2) directed-force-agents." The `lo-` prefix attaches to `yom`, the first element of the number phrase; `wi-ra-li` (W217) is the patient head. W217 first attestation.

<span id="S673"></span>
**S673**
`la-Solomon  ka-be-ne  lo-wi-ra-li  lu-ko-mu`
*Solomon bound the spirits, confining them in a vessel.*

!!! annotation "Notes"
    W218 (`ka-be-ne`, deliberate bond-creation) first corpus attestation. `lu-ko-mu` uses the result/beneficiary slot as a spatial destination: the vessel is what the binding produces/fills. The antonym `ka-ne-de` (W092, deliberate bond dissolution) gives the full bind/unbind pair.

<span id="S674"></span>
**S674**
`la-Solomon  ka-fe-si  lo-ko-mu  go-si`
*Solomon sealed the vessel with his sign.*

!!! annotation "Notes"
    `ka-fe-si` = deliberately-impose-boundary-signal = seal (compositional: `ka` + `fe-si` W024; no registry entry). `go-si` = "by means of / originating from signal" = instrumental use of `go-`. The causal origin of the sealing is Solomon's sign/mark; agent identity makes `si` unambiguous.

<span id="S675"></span>
**S675**
`la-li-Bavel  se-ka  lo-ko-mu`
*The Babylonians found the vessel through investigation.*

!!! annotation "Notes"
    `li-Bavel` = person-of-Bavel = Babylonian (Bavel = Hebrew name for Babylon). Group NP with `la-` agent prefix: Babylonians-as-agent. W034 (`se-ka`, examination/deliberate inspection) fits: the Babylonians were excavating the buried pit deliberately, not stumbling upon it.

<span id="S676"></span>
**S676**
`la-li-Bavel  ka-no-ko  lo-ko-mu`
*The Babylonians opened the vessel.*

!!! annotation "Notes"
    `ka-no-ko` = deliberately-act-toward-no-containment = open a sealed container. Compositional: `ka` (deliberate) + `no` (negation) + `ko` (containment/interior). No new vocabulary required.

<span id="S677"></span>
**S677**
`la-wi-ra-li  ki  go-ka-no-ko'ko-mu`
*The spirits moved forth, [released by] the opening of the vessel.*

!!! annotation "Notes"
    `go-ka-no-ko'ko-mu` = "caused by [the opening]-of-[the vessel]." First `go-[action'object]` causal nominalization in the corpus: `'` juncture binds the action compound `ka-no-ko` to its object `ko-mu`; `go-` takes the resulting nominalized event as its causal argument. `ki` (motion, primitive) — direction unspecified; narrative context supplies the outward reading.
