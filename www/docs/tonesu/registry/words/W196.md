## Composition

pa (space/place) + ne (relation/connection) + su (structure/order). Right-branching: pa-(ne-su). ne-su = relational structure (the kind of structure constituted by connections/relations). pa-(ne-su) = the spatial system constituted by those relational structures = the Great Wheel. Parse note: right-branching is mandatory. The wrong parse (pa-ne)-su = structure of spatial-relations would invert the modifier/head relationship.

## Definition

cosmological framework; multiverse; the Great Wheel of D&D as a structured system of planes and their relations; any system of spaces constituted by their structural relations to each other.

## Notes

W196. First attested DND-003 (S619, the Great Wheel). pa-ne-su captures the Great Wheel's defining character as a relational system: the planes are defined by their structural relations to each other (alignment positions, transitive connections, elemental adjacencies), not by any single property. The term generalizes beyond D&D to any multiverse or multi-domain cosmological framework.
