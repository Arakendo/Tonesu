## Composition

pa (place/space) + ki (motion) — motion-through-space

## Definition

Stative predicate: an entity is in uncontrolled spatial motion, moving through space without directed propulsion. In sentence position: `lo-X  pa-ki` = X drifts / X is adrift / X moves through space [without control]. The `pa` head grounds the motion as spatial (place-relative); `ki` specifies motion as the property.

## Notes

Distinct from `ki` alone (inchoative motion, often directed) and `ki-pa` (W049, motion-place = corridor = a structured path). `pa-ki` is the stative compound for existing adrift in space — unstructured, ongoing, spatial motion.
