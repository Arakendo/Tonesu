## Composition

ra (force/energy) + ka (intentional action) + li (social agent). Head: li (agent-type). Modifier: ra-ka (force-action) = purposeful application of force. Compare ra-ki (W038, storm/non-volitional energy-in-motion): the ka root IS the distinction — a ra-ka-li acts with deliberate force; ra-ki is non-volitional natural force. Right-branching: ra-(ka-li) = force-kind-of-action-person; li as derivational head marks agent role.

## Notes

W206. First attested DND-004 (S651). D&D Classes batch. The fighter is the canonical force-action agent: their defining compound is the simplest in the class set (no metaphysical source required, unlike wizard/cleric/paladin). Cross-class contrast: ra-ka-li (fighter) and vo-ra-li (W209, cleric) both mobilize ra, but fighter's source is direct ka (volitional effort); cleric's source is vo (sacred worth). Barbarian (not filed) would contrast as fa-ra-li (affect-force-person), where the force is reactive/emotional rather than intentional.
