## Definition

Passover; the annual liberation festival. The historically recurring deliberate liberation event. `ka-pa-ki` (W160, liberation act = deliberate place-motion) + `'ti-re` (W103, recurring cycle). The juncture mark is required: `ka-pa-ki` is the pre-bound liberation-act unit; `ti-re` adds the annual recurrence. Written: `kapaki'tire`.

## Notes

Passover encoded as the recurring liberation act: each year's festival is a re-enactment of ka-pa-ki (the Exodus liberation, W160). This grounds the Last Supper's Passover context: Jesus' last supper IS the Passover meal — ka-pa-ki'ti-re is both the events-of-the-night category and the founding liberation it commemorates.
Connection to EXO-001: ka-pa-ki (W160) was the Exodus liberation act. ka-pa-ki'ti-re adds the liturgical recurrence dimension at Sinai.
