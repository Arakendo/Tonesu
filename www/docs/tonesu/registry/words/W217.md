## Composition

wi (will/intention) + ra (force/energy) + li (social agent/person) = will-force-agent. Three-root cascade: motivation (wi) → power (ra) → agent-status (li). Right-branch parse: wi-[ra-li] = will-[force-agent] = a wilful being of force.

## Definition

spirit; directed-force-agent. An intentional entity whose nature is constituted by willed force — a non-human agent defined by its capacity to exercise directed power. Covers Solomonic spirits and demons in the commanding-and-binding tradition; powerful intentional beings whose defining attribute is the exercise of willed force rather than biological nature (zo-li = person) or artifactual agency (mu-li = AI/institution). Distinct from si-ki-li (W158: messenger/angel — defined by mission and signal) and wi-de-li (W124: adversary — defined by opposition to another). A wi-ra-li may be commanded, bound, and released; its nature is power, not enmity.

## Notes

SOL-001 first attestation (S672). Introduced with the Ars Goetia binding narrative: the 72 entities Solomon commanded are the paradigm case. Distinct from si-ki-li (W158: messengers defined by mission) and wi-de-li (W124: adversaries defined by opposition). Neutral as to benevolence: wi-ra-li is not equivalent to de-vo (W121: evil, value-corruption quality); many Solomonic spirits are neither good nor evil in moral terms.
