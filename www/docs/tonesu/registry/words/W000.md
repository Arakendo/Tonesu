## Composition

to (pattern/thought) + ne (relation/connection) + su (structure/order)

## Definition

truth; complete structured knowledge; the language itself. The word means both simultaneously and by design: the language is named after its own aspiration.
to-ne-su = the condition of patterns held together in structure = truth as an organized relational whole, not a proposition but a state of coherent knowledge.

## Notes

The language name and the word for truth are the same word. This is intentional:
speaking Tonesu is an act of reaching toward truth; the language is named after what it aspires to express.
Dual use is not ambiguity — in any sentence, "tonesu" names either the language (used as a proper noun) or the concept (used as a common noun); context resolves the reading without structural change.
The ~tonesu derivation is particularly useful: ~tonesu = approximately truth = a theory, a hypothesis, a working model. The approximation operator applied to the word for truth gives you conjecture. This is available without any new vocabulary and captures the epistemic gradient from ~tonesu (conjecture) through si (hypothesis) to to-ne-su (established truth / organized knowledge).
Compression: tonesu follows the same middle-drop pattern as zol/zof — to + [ne dropped contextually] + su → tonesu. First documented compression in the language.

## Related
