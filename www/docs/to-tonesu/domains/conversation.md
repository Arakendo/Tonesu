# Casual Conversation

*How Tonesu handles everyday social interaction — greetings, small talk, hedging, register, humor, and the phatic texture of real speech.*

Formal sentences are the easy case. Casual conversation is where vocabulary gaps become visible: the words that oil social interaction, express uncertainty without full evidential machinery, soften a request, or mark that something was a joke. The `fa-CON` corpus batch (affect in conversation) and C001–C008 conversations are the primary data.

---

## What formal Tonesu doesn't cover yet

**Phatic speech** — greetings, farewells, acknowledgments. Does Tonesu have a "hello"? A "thank you"? These are speech acts with social function but low propositional content. Do they reduce to existing roots, or are they gaps?

**Hedging and softening** — `~` (approximation) handles epistemic hedging of quantities and claims, but social softening is different: *I was sort of thinking maybe we could...* is not an approximation claim, it's a face-saving act. What carries that?

**Register** — written Tonesu is formal by default. What signals informality? The colloquial contracted forms in `registry/colloquial.md` are a start. Is there a register system, or just a vocabulary gap?

**Humor and irony** — irony requires the listener to infer the negation of the surface meaning. Tonesu's explicit denial machinery (`no —`) is the wrong tool. What marks non-literal speech?

**Back-channeling** — *mm*, *right*, *I see*, *go on*. Does Tonesu have minimal response tokens? The `ya` (attention-signal) particle is close, but it's forward-pointing, not a listener acknowledgment.

## Starting points from the corpus

| Batch | What it tests |
|-------|--------------|
| `fa-CON` | Affect registration across conversation (anger, grief, rising concern) |
| `C001`–`C008` | Eight full conversations covering coordination, dispute, social, theological, legal contexts |
| `CVP-001` | Vocative and direct address — `he na-Moses!` |
| `ODL-001` | Embodied states — hunger, fatigue — the most everyday of needs |
