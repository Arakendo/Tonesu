# Tonesu

**A compositional language designed to support clearer thinking.**

Words in Tonesu are built from primitive concepts rather than memorised as arbitrary vocabulary.
Meaning emerges from structure: by combining a small set of roots according to clear rules, speakers can construct and interpret new ideas without needing a dictionary.

---

## How it works

Tonesu words follow a consistent compositional pattern:

- **Primitive roots** represent fundamental concepts — around 34 of them
- **Compounding** builds more complex ideas from those roots
- **Operator patterns** allow certain roots to transform meaning in predictable ways

For example:

```
to-su  =  knowledge + structure  →  organised knowledge, a theory
```

```
to-li  =  knowledge + person    →  knower, scholar
```

A speaker who knows the primitives can interpret an unfamiliar compound simply by reading its structure.

---

## Designed for clarity

Tonesu is not just about vocabulary. Its grammar includes tools designed to make reasoning more transparent:

- Explicit **role marking** so every participant's relationship to the action is stated
- **Evidential framing** to separate direct claims from reported or inferred ones
- A **topic marker** to keep arguments focused
- An **approximation mark** for hedged or estimated claims

The goal is not to replace natural language, but to provide a system where ideas can be expressed with less ambiguity and less rhetorical noise.

---

## A living research project

Tonesu is an open exploration in compositional language design, ontology and conceptual structure, and discourse clarity. The project includes a growing primitive ontology, a formal grammar specification, corpus experiments, and an evolving word registry.

The name says it all: **to-ne-su** — *pattern, relation, structure* — truth as organised knowledge. The language is named after what it aspires to express.
